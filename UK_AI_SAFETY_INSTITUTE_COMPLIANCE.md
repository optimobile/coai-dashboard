## 1. UK AI Safety Institute Standards Integration

### 1.1 Core Safety Principles
The CSOAI platform aligns with UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) core principles:

- **Technical Evaluation** - All AI systems undergo rigorous safety evaluation
- **Risk Assessment** - Comprehensive evaluation of AI capabilities and risks
- **Mitigation Strategies** - Implementation of safeguards and controls
- **Transparency** - Clear documentation of AI decision-making processes
- **Accountability** - Human oversight and responsibility for AI decisions

### 1.2 Autonomous Systems Evaluation Standard
CSOAI implements the UK AISI Autonomous Systems Evaluation Standard:

- **Evaluation Requirements** - Minimal requirements for AI system assessment
- **Documentation** - Complete submission of evaluation data
- **Testing Protocols** - Standardized testing procedures
- **Risk Categorization** - Classification of AI risks by severity
- **Mitigation Verification** - Proof of safety control implementation

### 3.1 UK Approach to AI Regulation

The UK prioritizes a **flexible, pro-innovation framework** with sector-specific oversight:

- **Principle-Based** - Flexible principles rather than prescriptive rules
- **Sector-Specific** - Tailored requirements for different industries
- **Risk-Based** - Focus on high-risk AI applications
- **Collaborative** - Partnership between government, industry, and academia

## 4. Legal Terms & Conditions

### 4.1 Terms of Service - UK AI Safety Compliance

**1. AI System Usage**
- Users acknowledge that CSOAI uses AI systems evaluated per UK AISI standards
- All AI decisions are subject to human review and appeal
- Users have the right to request human review of any decision

**2. Data Protection & Privacy**
- All personal data processed in accordance with UK GDPR
- Data retention limited to necessary purposes
- Users have rights to access, correct, and delete data
- No data sold to third parties without explicit consent

**3. Liability & Limitations**
- CSOAI provides AI systems "as is" with no warranty
- CSOAI not liable for indirect or consequential damages
- Users responsible for verifying AI recommendations
- Maximum liability limited to fees paid in past 12 months

**4. Intellectual Property**
- Course content licensed under Creative Commons Attribution
- User-generated content remains user property
- CSOAI retains rights to platform and algorithms

**5. Dispute Resolution**
- Disputes resolved through UK courts
- Arbitration available for commercial disputes
- Mediation required before litigation

### 4.2 Privacy Policy - UK AI Safety Compliance

**1. Data Collection**
- Personal data collected only with explicit consent
- Collection limited to necessary purposes
- Users informed of all data uses

**2. Data Processing**
- Data processed in accordance with UK GDPR
- Data processed only for stated purposes
- Data not transferred outside UK/EU without safeguards
- Data subject to UK data protection laws

**3. AI Processing**
- Users informed when AI systems process their data
- Users have right to human review of AI decisions
- Users can opt-out of AI processing where possible
- AI decisions subject to appeal process

**4. Data Retention**
- Personal data retained only as long as necessary
- Inactive accounts deleted after 2 years
- User can request deletion at any time
- Deletion completed within 30 days

**5. User Rights**
- Right to access personal data
- Right to correct inaccurate data
- Right to delete data (right to be forgotten)
- Right to data portability
- Right to object to processing
- Right to lodge complaint with ICO

**6. Security**
- Data encrypted in transit and at rest
- Regular security audits
- Incident response plan in place
- Breach notification within 72 hours

### 4.3 AI Decision Appeal Process

**1. Appeal Rights**
- Users can appeal any AI decision
- Appeals reviewed by human experts
- Appeals resolved within 14 days
- Users notified of appeal outcome

**2. Appeal Procedure**
1. User submits appeal request
2. Decision reviewed by certified analyst
3. If needed, escalated to Byzantine Council
4. Final decision communicated to user
5. User can request further review

**3. Remedies**
- Decision reversal if error found
- Compensation if harm caused
- System improvement to prevent recurrence
