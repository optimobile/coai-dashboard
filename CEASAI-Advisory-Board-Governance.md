# CEASA Advisory Board Governance System

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** December 27, 2025  
**Prepared by:** Manus AI  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive governance framework for CEASA Advisory Board and curriculum governance

---

## Executive Summary

The CEASA Advisory Board is the governing body responsible for strategic oversight, curriculum development, and quality assurance of the CEASA certification program. The board ensures that CEASA maintains its position as the **authoritative, independent training body for AI Safety Analysts** while serving the interests of all stakeholders: analysts, employers, regulators, and the public.

**Board Composition:** 15-21 members representing diverse expertise and stakeholder groups  
**Meeting Frequency:** Quarterly (4 meetings per year) + emergency sessions as needed  
**Decision-Making:** Consensus-based with voting procedures for curriculum changes  
**Transparency:** Public board member directory, published meeting minutes, public curriculum voting

## Part 1: Advisory Board Structure & Governance

### 1.1 Board Composition

The CEASA Advisory Board comprises 15-21 members representing diverse expertise, geographic regions, and stakeholder groups.

#### **Board Member Categories**

**1. Academic & Research Experts (4-5 members)**
- [ ] Leading AI safety researchers from top universities
- [ ] Machine learning fairness and bias experts
- [ ] AI governance and policy researchers
- [ ] Minimum 10 years research experience
- [ ] Active publication record in AI safety

**2. Industry Practitioners (4-5 members)**
- [ ] Chief AI Officers from major enterprises
- [ ] AI safety leads from healthcare, finance, autonomous systems
- [ ] Practitioners with 10+ years AI safety experience
- [ ] Diverse industry sectors represented
- [ ] Active involvement in AI safety initiatives

**3. Regulatory & Government Representatives (3-4 members)**
- [ ] Representatives from CSOAI
- [ ] EU AI Act implementation experts
- [ ] National AI regulatory bodies
- [ ] International AI governance experts
- [ ] Minimum 5 years regulatory experience

**4. Civil Society & Ethics Experts (2-3 members)**
- [ ] AI ethics and fairness advocates
- [ ] Civil rights and privacy advocates
- [ ] Consumer protection representatives
- [ ] Diversity and inclusion experts
- [ ] Public interest representation

**5. Professional & Certification Bodies (1-2 members)**
- [ ] Representatives from ISO/IEC standards bodies
- [ ] Accreditation body representatives
- [ ] Professional certification organization leaders
- [ ] Quality assurance experts

**6. CEASA Leadership (1-2 members)**
- [ ] CEASA Executive Director
- [ ] CEASA Chief Curriculum Officer
- [ ] Non-voting or limited voting participation

### 1.2 Board Member Selection

**Selection Criteria:**
- Demonstrated expertise in AI safety, governance, or related field
- Minimum 5 years professional experience
- Commitment to CEASA mission and values
- Ability to represent stakeholder interests impartially
- Geographic and demographic diversity
- No significant conflicts of interest

**Selection Process:**
- [ ] Nominations from existing board members, stakeholders, and public
- [ ] Nominations committee review (3 board members)
- [ ] Candidate interviews and background checks
- [ ] Board vote (2/3 majority required)
- [ ] Public announcement of new board members

**Term Limits:**
- Initial terms: 3 years (staggered to ensure continuity)
- Renewal terms: 3 years (maximum 2 consecutive terms, then 1-year break)
- Chair term: 2 years (maximum 2 consecutive terms)
- Vice-chair term: 2 years (maximum 2 consecutive terms)

### 1.3 Board Leadership

**Chair:**
- [ ] Elected by board members (2-year term)
- [ ] Presides over board meetings
- [ ] Represents CEASA publicly
- [ ] Chairs curriculum voting procedures
- [ ] Liaison with accreditation bodies
- [ ] Selection: Board vote (2/3 majority)

**Vice-Chair:**
- [ ] Elected by board members (2-year term)
- [ ] Assumes chair duties if chair unavailable
- [ ] Supports chair in governance activities
- [ ] Chairs specific board committees
- [ ] Selection: Board vote (2/3 majority)

**Secretary:**
- [ ] Appointed by board (2-year term)
- [ ] Maintains board records and minutes
- [ ] Schedules meetings and communications
- [ ] Manages board documentation
- [ ] Ensures GDPR compliance

**Treasurer:**
- [ ] Appointed by board (2-year term)
- [ ] Oversees CEASA finances
- [ ] Prepares financial reports
- [ ] Manages budget and resource allocation
- [ ] Ensures financial transparency

### 1.4 Board Committees

**Curriculum Committee (5-7 members)**
- Reviews and recommends curriculum changes
- Evaluates new modules and specializations
- Ensures EU AI Act alignment
- Conducts annual curriculum review
- Recommends curriculum updates to full board

**Quality Assurance Committee (3-5 members)**
- Monitors exam quality and fairness
- Reviews assessment procedures
- Analyzes pass rates and performance data
- Recommends improvements
- Ensures ISO 17024 compliance

**Stakeholder Engagement Committee (3-5 members)**
- Collects feedback from analysts, employers, regulators
- Conducts annual stakeholder surveys
- Organizes stakeholder forums
- Communicates board decisions to stakeholders
- Represents diverse stakeholder interests

**Accreditation & Compliance Committee (3-5 members)**
- Monitors accreditation body requirements
- Ensures compliance with ISO 17024 and ISO 17065
- Prepares for annual accreditation assessments
- Manages corrective actions
- Liaisons with accreditation bodies

**Finance & Operations Committee (3-5 members)**
- Oversees CEASA budget and finances
- Ensures operational efficiency
- Reviews staffing and resource needs
- Recommends strategic investments
- Manages risk and business continuity

**Financial Oversight:**
- [ ] Approve annual budget
- [ ] Monitor financial performance

### 2.2 Decision-Making Procedures

**Quorum:**
- Minimum 2/3 of board members present for decisions
- Remote participation allowed via video conference
- Proxy voting not permitted

**Voting Procedures:**
- Simple majority (>50%) for routine decisions
- 2/3 majority for curriculum changes
- 2/3 majority for board member selection
- 2/3 majority for strategic decisions
- Unanimous consent for policy changes affecting accreditation

**Curriculum Voting Procedures:**
- Curriculum Committee recommends changes
- Full board discussion (minimum 2 meetings)
- Stakeholder comment period (30 days minimum)
- Board vote on curriculum changes
- Public announcement of decisions

**Conflict of Interest:**
- Board members disclose conflicts before voting
- Members with conflicts abstain from voting
- Conflicts documented in meeting minutes
- Conflicts reviewed annually

**Meeting Minutes:**
- Detailed minutes of all board meetings
- Decisions and voting records documented
- Conflicts of interest noted
- Public availability (within 30 days)
- Retained for minimum 5 years

### 2.3 Curriculum Governance Process

**Annual Curriculum Review:**
- [ ] Curriculum Committee conducts comprehensive review
- [ ] Analyzes candidate feedback and performance data
- [ ] Reviews EU AI Act updates and regulatory changes
- [ ] Identifies curriculum gaps and improvement opportunities
- [ ] Recommends changes to full board

**Curriculum Change Procedures:**
1. **Proposal Phase** - Committee develops proposal with rationale
2. **Discussion Phase** - Board discusses proposal (minimum 2 meetings)
3. **Stakeholder Comment Period** - Public 30-day comment period
4. **Board Vote** - Vote on curriculum change (2/3 majority required)
5. **Implementation** - Changes implemented with transition period
6. **Monitoring** - Track effectiveness of changes

**New Module Development:**
- [ ] Curriculum Committee identifies need for new module
- [ ] Subject matter experts develop module content
- [ ] Pilot testing with sample candidates
- [ ] Stakeholder feedback collection
- [ ] Board approval (2/3 majority)
- [ ] Integration into curriculum

**Specialization Track Development:**
- [ ] Industry experts propose new specialization
- [ ] Curriculum Committee evaluates proposal
- [ ] Develops specialization curriculum
- [ ] Stakeholder consultation
- [ ] Board approval (2/3 majority)
- [ ] Launch and monitoring

## Part 5: Curriculum Governance Framework

### 5.1 Curriculum Change Categories

**Minor Changes (Curriculum Committee approval only):**
- Updates to module content (no change to learning outcomes)
- Updates to case studies or examples
- Corrections to errors or outdated information
- Updates to references and resources
- Implementation: Immediate or next offering

**Moderate Changes (Board vote, 2/3 majority):**
- New modules or specializations
- Changes to module learning outcomes
- Changes to assessment procedures
- Changes to passing score
- Changes to prerequisite requirements
- Implementation: 60-90 days notice to candidates

**Major Changes (Board vote, 2/3 majority + stakeholder consultation):**
- Changes to tier structure or progression
- Changes to certification requirements
- Changes to exam format or duration
- Changes to certificate validity period
- Changes to continuing education requirements
- Implementation: 90-180 days notice to candidates

### 5.2 Curriculum Change Process

**Step 1: Identification (Curriculum Committee)**
- Identify need for curriculum change
- Analyze stakeholder feedback
- Review regulatory updates
- Assess impact on candidates and employers

**Step 2: Development (Subject Matter Experts)**
- Develop proposed changes
- Prepare rationale and justification
- Identify implementation timeline
- Assess resource requirements

**Step 3: Committee Review (Curriculum Committee)**
- Review proposal and rationale
- Assess alignment with EU AI Act
- Evaluate impact on candidates
- Recommend to full board

**Step 4: Board Discussion (Full Board)**
- Present proposal to board
- Board discussion (minimum 2 meetings)
- Address questions and concerns
- Refine proposal as needed

**Step 5: Stakeholder Consultation (30 days)**
- Publish proposed changes
- Collect feedback from analysts, employers, regulators
- Analyze feedback
- Make adjustments based on feedback

**Step 6: Board Vote (Full Board)**
- Vote on curriculum change
- 2/3 majority required for approval
- Document voting results
- Announce decision publicly

**Step 7: Implementation**
- Communicate changes to candidates
- Update curriculum materials
- Train instructors and proctors
- Monitor implementation
- Collect feedback on changes

### 5.3 Curriculum Review Cycle

**Annual Curriculum Review (Every year):**
- [ ] Curriculum Committee comprehensive review
- [ ] Analysis of candidate feedback and performance
- [ ] Review of regulatory updates
- [ ] Identification of curriculum gaps
- [ ] Recommendations for improvements
- [ ] Board discussion and decisions

**Triennial Comprehensive Review (Every 3 years):**
- [ ] External expert evaluation of curriculum
- [ ] Benchmarking against international standards
- [ ] Assessment of alignment with industry needs
- [ ] Evaluation of specialization tracks
- [ ] Strategic curriculum planning
- [ ] Board approval of strategic direction

**Regulatory Alignment Review (As needed):**
- [ ] Monitor EU AI Act updates and amendments
- [ ] Assess impact on curriculum
- [ ] Identify required changes
- [ ] Implement changes to maintain compliance
- [ ] Communicate updates to stakeholders
