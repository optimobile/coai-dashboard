# How to Submit a Watchdog Report

This guide explains how to report AI safety incidents through the CSOAI Watchdog program.

## What is the Watchdog Program?

The Watchdog program is a transparent, public database of AI safety incidents. Anyone can submit reports about AI systems that:

- Exhibit bias or discrimination
- Make harmful decisions
- Violate privacy
- Lack transparency
- Cause unintended harm

## Why Report AI Incidents?

Your reports help:
- **Protect the public** from harmful AI systems
- **Hold companies accountable** for AI safety
- **Improve AI systems** through feedback
- **Build a public database** of AI risks
- **Train AI Safety Analysts** with real cases

## Step 1: Access the Watchdog Portal

1. Visit [csoai.org/watchdog](https://csoai.org/watchdog)
2. You can submit reports anonymously or logged in
3. Logged-in users can track their report status

## Step 2: Describe the Incident

Fill out the report form with:

### Required Information:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **AI System Name** | The name of the AI system (e.g., "ChatGPT", "Hiring AI Tool") |
| **Company/Organization** | Who operates the AI system |
| **Incident Type** | Category: Bias, Privacy, Safety, Transparency, Other |
| **Description** | Detailed description of what happened |
| **Impact** | Who was affected and how |

### Optional Information:

- **Evidence** - Screenshots, links, documentation
- **Date of Incident** - When it occurred
- **Your Contact** - For follow-up (only if not anonymous)

## Step 3: Submit Your Report

1. Review your report for accuracy
2. Click **"Submit Report"**
3. Receive a confirmation with your report ID
4. Track status in the public Watchdog feed

## What Happens After Submission?

### 1. Initial Review
Your report enters the public database and is visible to all users.

### 2. Analyst Assignment
A certified AI Safety Analyst reviews your report for:
- Validity and accuracy
- Severity assessment
- Framework violations (EU AI Act, NIST, etc.)

### 3. Council Review
Significant cases go to the 33-Agent Byzantine Council for:
- Multi-perspective analysis
- Consensus decision-making
- Recommendations for action

### 4. Public Transparency
All decisions and outcomes are published publicly, including:
- Analyst findings
- Council votes
- Recommended actions

## Report Categories

### Bias & Discrimination
AI systems that treat people unfairly based on:
- Race, gender, age
- Disability, religion
- Socioeconomic status

**Example:** A hiring AI that rejects qualified candidates based on gender.

### Privacy Violations
AI systems that:
- Collect data without consent
- Share personal information
- Track users inappropriately

**Example:** A facial recognition system used without user knowledge.

### Safety Concerns
AI systems that:
- Make dangerous decisions
- Lack proper safeguards
- Cause physical or financial harm

**Example:** An autonomous vehicle making unsafe decisions.

### Transparency Issues
AI systems that:
- Don't explain decisions
- Hide their AI nature
- Lack accountability

**Example:** A chatbot pretending to be human.

## Tips for Effective Reports

1. **Be specific** - Include dates, names, and details
2. **Provide evidence** - Screenshots and documentation help
3. **Explain impact** - Who was affected and how
4. **Stay factual** - Avoid speculation
5. **Follow up** - Monitor your report status

## Anonymous Reporting

You can submit reports anonymously:
- No account required
- No personal information collected
- Report still enters public database
- Cannot track status without account

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will my identity be revealed?**
A: Anonymous reports remain anonymous. Logged-in reports show username only if you choose.

**Q: What if my report is rejected?**
A: Reports are rarely rejected. If yours is, you'll receive an explanation and can resubmit.

**Q: Can I report my employer's AI system?**
A: Yes. Whistleblower protections apply in many jurisdictions.

**Q: How long until my report is reviewed?**
A: Most reports are reviewed within 48-72 hours.

**Q: Can I update my report?**
A: Yes. Logged-in users can add information to their reports.
