# CEASA Enhanced Curriculum Structure: 3-Tier System with Specialization Tracks

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** December 27, 2025  
**Prepared by:** Manus AI  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive curriculum design for CEASA certification program positioning analysts as authoritative figures in AI safety governance

---

## Executive Summary

The Certified EU AI Safety Analyst (CEASA) program is structured as a **progressive 3-tier certification system** with **4 specialization tracks**, designed to create authoritative AI safety professionals capable of assessing, auditing, and certifying high-risk AI systems under EU AI Act requirements.

**Program Structure:**
- **Fundamentals Tier:** 12 modules (120 hours) - Foundation knowledge
- **Professional Tier:** 30 modules (300 hours) - Operational expertise
- **Expert Tier:** 40+ modules (400+ hours) - Leadership authority
- **Specialization Tracks:** 6 modules each (60 hours) - Domain expertise

**Progression Path:** Fundamentals → Professional → Expert (+ optional specialization at any tier)

**Certification Value:** Graduates positioned as **authoritative figures capable of:**
- Conducting high-risk AI system compliance audits
- Assessing risk management systems (Article 9)
- Evaluating data governance and bias mitigation (Article 10)
- Verifying transparency and human oversight (Articles 13-14)
- Supporting regulatory compliance across all 8 Annex III categories
- Advising enterprises on EU AI Act compliance
- Contributing to CSOAI's oversight and incident analysis

## Part 1: Fundamentals Tier (12 Modules, 120 Hours)

The Fundamentals tier provides comprehensive foundation knowledge in AI safety, EU AI Act requirements, and basic compliance assessment skills.

### Module 1: Introduction to AI Safety & Governance

**Duration:** 10 hours  
**Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand AI safety as a discipline and its importance
- Recognize key stakeholders in AI governance (regulators, companies, researchers, civil society)
- Explain the role of CSOAI and CEASA in the AI safety ecosystem
- Identify major AI safety incidents and lessons learned

**Content:**
- AI safety fundamentals and risk categories
- Historical AI incidents and their consequences
- Regulatory landscape overview (EU, US, China, international)
- CSOAI mission and the FAA-for-AI model
- CEASA role in training and certification
- Career pathways for AI safety professionals

**Assessment:** Multiple-choice exam (20 questions) + reflection paper on AI safety incident

### Module 2: EU AI Act Overview & Architecture

**Duration:** 10 hours  
**Learning Outcomes:**
- Explain the EU AI Act's risk-based approach
- Identify the four risk levels (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal)
- Understand the relationship between Annex I and Annex III
- Recognize key articles and their purposes

**Content:**
- EU AI Act structure and timeline
- Risk classification framework
- Prohibited AI practices (Article 5)
- High-risk AI systems (Article 6, Annex III)
- Transparency obligations (Article 50)
- Enforcement and penalties (Articles 83-91)
- Comparison with NIST AI RMF and TC260 frameworks

**Assessment:** Multiple-choice exam (25 questions) + case study analysis

### Module 3: High-Risk AI System Classification (Annex III)

**Duration:** 15 hours  
**Learning Outcomes:**
- Classify AI systems into the 8 Annex III categories
- Determine high-risk vs. non-high-risk status
- Document risk assessment decisions
- Identify derogation conditions

**Content:**
- Deep dive into each of the 8 Annex III categories:
  1. Biometrics (remote ID, emotion recognition)
  2. Critical infrastructure (power, water, traffic)
  3. Education (admissions, assessment)
  4. Employment (recruitment, monitoring)
  5. Essential services (healthcare, credit, insurance)
  6. Law enforcement (criminal risk, profiling)
  7. Migration & border control
  8. Justice & democracy
- Article 6 derogation conditions
- Risk assessment documentation
- Provider responsibilities

**Assessment:** Classification exercise with 20 real-world AI system scenarios + written justification

### Module 4: Risk Management Systems (Article 9)

**Duration:** 15 hours  
**Learning Outcomes:**
- Understand continuous risk management lifecycle
- Identify known and foreseeable risks
- Evaluate risk mitigation measures
- Assess residual risk acceptability

**Content:**

- Risk identification and analysis procedures
- Risk estimation and evaluation methods
- Risk mitigation strategies

- Testing and validation protocols
- Documentation requirements

**Assessment:** Risk management plan development for a sample AI system + exam (20 questions)

## Part 5: Progression & Prerequisites

### Tier Progression Requirements

**Fundamentals → Professional:**
- Complete Fundamentals tier (all 12 modules)
- Pass Fundamentals certification exam (70%+)
- Minimum 6 months professional experience or equivalent
- Professional tier application and approval

**Professional → Expert:**
- Complete Professional tier (all 30 modules)
- Pass Professional certification exam (70%+)
- Minimum 2 years professional experience as certified analyst
- Expert tier application and approval
- Recommendation from supervisor or mentor

- Specialization track application and approval

**At Professional Level:**

- Specialization track application and approval

**At Expert Level:**
