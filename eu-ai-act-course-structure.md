## EU AI Act Fundamentals Course

### Course Overview
This foundational course introduces the EU AI Act, the world's first comprehensive AI regulation. Students will learn the risk-based classification system, prohibited practices, high-risk requirements, and compliance timelines. Perfect for business leaders, product managers, and anyone working with AI systems in the EU market.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Explain the EU AI Act's purpose, scope, and regulatory framework** including its risk-based approach and governance structure
2. **Classify AI systems** according to the four risk categories (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal)
3. **Identify prohibited AI practices** and understand the legal consequences of deploying banned systems
4. **Recognize high-risk AI systems** using Annex III use cases and understand when conformity assessment is required
5. **Describe provider and deployer obligations** for high-risk AI systems at a high level
6. **Understand compliance timelines and penalties** to plan organizational readiness
7. **Apply basic compliance principles** to real-world AI system scenarios

### Module Structure (8 modules, ~60 minutes each)

#### Module 1: Introduction to the EU AI Act
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Why the EU AI Act was created
- Scope and territorial application
- Key definitions (AI system, provider, deployer, placing on market)
- Relationship to other EU regulations (GDPR, Product Safety Directive)
- Global impact and Brussels Effect

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Understand the regulatory context and drivers behind the AI Act
- Identify when the AI Act applies to your organization
- Define key terms used throughout the regulation

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions

---

#### Module 2: Risk Classification System
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Four-tier risk pyramid (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal)
- Risk classification criteria (Article 6)
- Annex I: Safety components requiring third-party assessment
- Annex III: High-risk use cases
- How to determine your AI system's risk level

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Classify AI systems into the correct risk category
- Understand the criteria that make a system high-risk
- Navigate Annexes I and III effectively

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions + 2 case study scenarios

---

#### Module 3: Prohibited AI Practices
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Complete list of banned AI systems (Article 5)
- Subliminal manipulation and exploitation of vulnerabilities
- Social scoring systems
- Biometric categorization for sensitive attributes
- Predictive policing based solely on profiling
- Emotion recognition in workplaces and schools
- Real-time remote biometric identification (and narrow exceptions)
- Enforcement timeline (6 months after entry into force)

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Identify prohibited AI practices
- Understand the narrow exceptions for law enforcement biometrics
- Recognize compliance deadline (February 2025)

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions

---

#### Module 4: High-Risk AI Systems - Part 1
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Annex III use cases deep dive:
  - Biometrics (non-banned)

  - Employment and worker management
- Real-world examples in each category
- Exceptions to high-risk classification

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Recognize high-risk AI systems in biometrics, infrastructure, education, and employment
- Apply exceptions correctly (narrow procedural tasks, human activity improvement)
- Evaluate whether your AI system falls into these categories

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions + 2 case studies

---

#### Module 5: High-Risk AI Systems - Part 2
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Annex III use cases continued:
  - Essential public and private services
  - Law enforcement
  - Migration, asylum, border control
  - Administration of justice and democratic processes
- Profiling and its implications
- When self-assessment is sufficient vs. third-party required

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Recognize high-risk AI systems in public services, law enforcement, migration, and justice
- Understand profiling rules
- Determine conformity assessment pathway

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions + 2 case studies

---

#### Module 6: Provider and Deployer Obligations
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Provider (developer) obligations overview:
  - Risk management system
  - Data governance
  - Technical documentation
  - Record-keeping and logging
  - Instructions for use
  - Human oversight design
  - Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity
  - Quality management system
- Deployer (user) obligations overview:
  - Following instructions for use
  - Implementing human oversight
  - Monitoring operation
  - Reporting serious incidents
- Shared responsibilities in the AI value chain

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Understand the 8 core provider obligations
- Recognize deployer responsibilities
- Identify your role in the AI value chain

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions

---

#### Module 7: Transparency, GPAI, and Governance
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Transparency obligations for limited-risk AI (chatbots, deepfakes)
- General Purpose AI (GPAI) models and systems
- GPAI obligations (documentation, copyright, training data summary)
- Systemic risk GPAI (>10^25 FLOPs)
- Governance structure:
  - AI Office
  - National competent authorities
  - European AI Board
  - Scientific panel
- CE marking and conformity assessment

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Apply transparency requirements to chatbots and generative AI
- Understand GPAI obligations for foundation models
- Navigate the governance structure
- Recognize CE marking requirements

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions

---

#### Module 8: Compliance Timeline, Penalties, and Next Steps
**Duration**: 60 minutes
**Topics**:
- Full implementation timeline:
  - Prohibited AI: 6 months (Feb 2025)
  - GPAI: 12 months (Aug 2025)
  - High-risk Annex III: 24 months (Aug 2026)
  - High-risk Annex I: 36 months (Aug 2027)
- Penalty structure:
  - €35M or 7% turnover: Prohibited practices
  - €15M or 3% turnover: High-risk violations
  - €7.5M or 1.5% turnover: Incorrect information
- SME adjustments
- Compliance roadmap for organizations
- Resources and next steps

**Learning Outcomes**:
- Plan compliance activities according to timeline
- Understand penalty risks
- Create a basic compliance roadmap for your organization

**Quiz**: 10 multiple-choice questions

---

## EU AI Act Advanced Course

### Course Overview
This advanced course provides in-depth, practical guidance for implementing EU AI Act compliance. Students will learn to conduct risk assessments, prepare technical documentation, establish data governance, implement human oversight, and navigate conformity assessment procedures. Ideal for compliance officers, AI project managers, and technical leads responsible for AI system deployment.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Conduct comprehensive risk assessments** for high-risk AI systems throughout their lifecycle
2. **Prepare technical documentation** that meets Article 11 requirements and enables authority assessment
3. **Implement data governance frameworks** ensuring training data is representative, accurate, and bias-free
4. **Design and deploy human oversight mechanisms** that enable effective intervention and control
5. **Navigate conformity assessment procedures** including self-assessment and third-party certification
6. **Establish post-market monitoring systems** for continuous compliance and incident reporting
7. **Apply compliance requirements** to sector-specific AI applications (healthcare, finance, HR, law enforcement)

## Assessment Philosophy

### Fundamentals Course
- **Focus**: Knowledge recall and basic application
- **Question Types**: Multiple choice, true/false, simple scenarios
- **Difficulty**: Entry-level
- **Passing**: 70%

### Advanced Course
- **Focus**: Practical application and implementation
- **Question Types**: Multiple choice, scenario-based, practical exercises
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Passing**: 75%

### Specialist Course
- **Focus**: Expert analysis and consulting
- **Question Types**: Complex scenarios, project work, professional deliverables
- **Difficulty**: Advanced/Expert
- **Passing**: 80%

## Continuous Learning

All certifications require renewal every 2 years through:
- Continuing education credits (20 hours)
- Re-examination (reduced 25-question exam)
- Proof of professional practice

This ensures certified professionals stay current with:
- Regulatory amendments
- Delegated acts and guidance
- Case law developments
- Best practice evolution
