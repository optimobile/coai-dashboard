# AI Safety Framework for Senegal: Building Tomorrow's AI Governance Today

## Executive Summary

Senegal stands at a pivotal moment in its technological evolution. With a burgeoning digital ecosystem and a government committed to digital transformation, the nation is poised to harness the immense potential of Artificial Intelligence (AI). However, this opportunity is accompanied by a critical challenge: the absence of a comprehensive AI safety and governance framework. As the European Union's AI Act deadline of February 2, 2026, approaches, the urgency for Senegal to establish its own regulatory landscape has never been greater. A proactive approach to AI governance will not only mitigate risks but also foster trust, attract investment, and ensure that AI is developed and deployed in a manner that is safe, ethical, and beneficial for all Senegalese citizens. This document outlines a clear path forward for Senegal to become a leader in AI governance in West Africa, leveraging the expertise and solutions offered by the Council of Safe AI (CSOAI).

## Current State of AI in Senegal

Senegal's journey into the digital age is marked by a vibrant and rapidly growing technology sector. The government's "Digital Senegal 2025" strategy has laid the groundwork for a digital-first economy, with significant investments in infrastructure, including data centers, 5G networks, and high-performance computing capabilities. This has fostered a dynamic ecosystem for digital entrepreneurship, making Senegal a hub for innovation in French-speaking West Africa. AI adoption is on the rise across various key industries. In the financial sector, AI is being deployed for fraud detection and risk management, enhancing the security and efficiency of financial services. The agricultural sector, a cornerstone of the Senegalese economy, is leveraging AI to improve crop yields and bolster food security through predictive analytics. Furthermore, the education sector is exploring AI-powered tools to enhance language learning and create more personalized educational experiences. The launch of the National Strategy for the Development of Artificial Intelligence (SNDIA) in 2023 underscores the government's commitment to integrating AI into the fabric of the nation's development. However, despite these advancements, challenges remain, including a relatively low investment in research and development, a significant digital divide between urban and rural areas, and the absence of a specific legal framework for AI.

## The AI Governance Gap

Senegal is one of 118 countries that have yet to establish a formal AI governance framework. This gap leaves the nation vulnerable to a range of risks associated with the rapid and unregulated proliferation of AI technologies. Without clear guidelines and oversight, there is a heightened risk of biased algorithms perpetuating and even amplifying existing social inequalities. The lack of a robust data protection framework in the context of AI also raises serious concerns about citizen privacy and the potential for misuse of personal data. For businesses, the absence of a clear regulatory environment creates uncertainty, hindering investment and innovation. The potential for AI-related job displacement and the spread of misinformation are other significant societal risks that need to be addressed through a comprehensive governance strategy. Closing this governance gap is not merely a matter of compliance; it is a strategic imperative for ensuring that AI serves as a catalyst for inclusive and sustainable development in Senegal.

## CSOAI's Solution for Senegal

The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) offers a comprehensive, white-label AI Safety Framework that can be tailored to Senegal's unique context and needs. This framework provides a clear and actionable roadmap for establishing a robust AI governance ecosystem. A key component of our solution is the CEASAI Certification, a professional qualification designed to build local capacity in AI safety and auditing. By training a cadre of certified AI safety analysts, Senegal can develop the in-house expertise needed to oversee the development and deployment of AI systems. The framework also includes the establishment of a 33-Agent Byzantine Council, an independent oversight body composed of diverse stakeholders, to ensure that AI is developed and used in a manner that is aligned with Senegalese values and priorities. Our SOAI-PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) compliance methodology provides a structured approach for organizations to continuously assess and improve their AI safety practices. This holistic solution empowers Senegal to not only meet international standards but also to become a leader in responsible AI innovation.

## Implementation Roadmap

CSOAI proposes a four-phased approach to the implementation of the AI Safety Framework in Senegal:

*   **Phase 1: Assessment and Planning (3 months):** This initial phase will involve a comprehensive assessment of Senegal's existing digital infrastructure, legal landscape, and key stakeholder needs. This will inform the customization of the AI Safety Framework to the specific context of Senegal.
*   **Phase 2: Framework Deployment (6 months):** In this phase, the tailored AI Safety Framework will be deployed, including the establishment of the 33-Agent Byzantine Council and the necessary regulatory and institutional mechanisms.
*   **Phase 3: Training 1,000 AI Safety Analysts:** This phase will focus on building local capacity through the training and certification of 1,000 AI Safety Analysts through the CEASAI program. This will create a sustainable pool of local talent to support the long-term implementation of the framework.
*   **Phase 4: Full Operational Capability:** In the final phase, the AI Safety Framework will be fully operational, with ongoing monitoring, evaluation, and refinement to ensure its continued effectiveness.
