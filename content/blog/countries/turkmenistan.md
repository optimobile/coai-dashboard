## Current State of AI in Turkmenistan

Turkmenistan is at the nascent stages of AI adoption, with the government demonstrating a clear commitment to digital transformation and the integration of modern technologies across various sectors. The country's leadership has expressed a strong interest in leveraging AI to drive economic growth and modernize public services. Recent initiatives have focused on exploring the potential of AI in key industries such as **energy, transportation, and agriculture**. The state-owned Turkmenaragatnashyk agency is actively seeking international partnerships to develop AI and cybersecurity capabilities. Furthermore, there is a growing emphasis on building local capacity, with several higher education institutions developing curricula in AI and blockchain technologies. The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) has also been active in the country, implementing AI for healthcare applications, such as using AI-powered X-ray analysis to screen for tuberculosis. While there are no comprehensive, publicly available statistics on the exact scale of AI adoption, these developments indicate a clear trajectory towards greater integration of AI in the coming years. The government's focus on creating a digital economy and its willingness to engage with international partners create a fertile ground for the adoption of a national AI safety framework.

## The AI Governance Gap

Turkmenistan is one of 118 countries that currently lack a formal AI governance framework. This 'governance gap' creates a precarious situation, exposing the nation to a multitude of risks. Without clear regulations and oversight, the development and deployment of AI systems can lead to unintended consequences, such as algorithmic bias, privacy violations, and security breaches. For citizens, this could mean a lack of transparency and accountability in how their data is used, and the potential for discriminatory outcomes in areas like employment and access to services. For businesses, the absence of a clear legal and ethical framework creates uncertainty, stifling innovation and deterring foreign investment. Furthermore, as international regulations like the EU AI Act come into force, Turkmen companies that use or develop AI may face significant barriers to accessing global markets. The lack of a national AI safety framework also presents a national security risk, as the country may become vulnerable to malicious AI-powered cyberattacks or the spread of disinformation. Closing this governance gap is not merely a matter of compliance; it is a strategic imperative for ensuring that AI is developed and used in a manner that is safe, ethical, and beneficial for all of Turkmenistan's citizens.
