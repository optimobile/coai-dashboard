**CSOAI Proposes National AI Safety Framework for Solomon Islands**

**[CITY, Solomon Islands] – [Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) today announced a proposal to partner with the government of the Solomon Islands to develop and implement a national AI Safety Framework. The proposed framework aims to establish a robust governance structure for Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the country, fostering innovation while mitigating potential risks.

“The Solomon Islands has a unique opportunity to build a world-class AI governance ecosystem from the ground up,” said [CSOAI Spokesperson Name], [Title] at CSOAI. “Our white-label AI Safety Framework, combined with our £1 billion global training initiative, will empower the Solomon Islands to become a leader in responsible AI in the Pacific region.”

The CSOAI’s proposal includes the training and certification of 1,000 local AI Safety Analysts through its globally recognized CEASAI program. This initiative will create a skilled workforce to manage and oversee the AI ecosystem, ensuring that AI is deployed safely and ethically.

“We are committed to working with the government and people of the Solomon Islands to build a prosperous and secure digital future,” added [CSOAI Spokesperson Name]. “Our proven SOAI-PDCA compliance methodology and innovative 33-Agent Byzantine Council for AI oversight will provide the tools and expertise needed to achieve this vision.”

## Key Messages

### AI Challenges in Solomon Islands
- Opportunity: Government's digital transformation focus creates a receptive environment for AI.
- Opportunity: Early AI adoption in education and climate prediction demonstrates a willingness to innovate.
- Challenge: Lack of a dedicated AI legal framework creates uncertainty and risk.
- Challenge: Limited local AI talent requires significant capacity building.
- Challenge: Infrastructure and resource constraints may hinder widespread AI implementation.
