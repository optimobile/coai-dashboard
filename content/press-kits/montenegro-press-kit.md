**CSOAI Launches Initiative to Bring AI Safety Framework to Montenegro**

**[City, State, Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) today announced a new initiative to help Montenegro build a comprehensive AI safety framework. The initiative comes as the European Union’s AI Act deadline of February 2, 2026, approaches, creating an urgent need for countries like Montenegro to align their legal and regulatory structures with EU standards.

“Montenegro is at a critical juncture in its digital transformation journey,” said [CSOAI Spokesperson Name], [Title] at CSOAI. “By taking a proactive approach to AI governance, Montenegro can position itself as a leader in the responsible development and deployment of AI, unlocking its full potential to drive innovation, economic growth, and social progress.”

CSOAI’s solution for Montenegro includes a white-label AI Safety Framework, the CEASAI certification program for local professionals, and a 33-Agent Byzantine Council for AI oversight. The framework is based on CSOAI’s proprietary SOAI-PDCA compliance methodology, which ensures that AI systems are developed and deployed in a safe, ethical, and transparent manner.

As part of the initiative, CSOAI is also offering free CEASAI certification training to Montenegrin citizens through its £1 billion training giveaway. This program will train 500,250 AI safety analysts worldwide, creating an immediate capacity for AI safety and oversight in Montenegro and around the world.

“We are committed to empowering individuals and communities to take control of their AI-powered future,” said [CSOAI Spokesperson Name]. “By providing free access to our world-class training, we are ensuring that AI is developed and deployed in a safe and ethical manner for the benefit of all.”
