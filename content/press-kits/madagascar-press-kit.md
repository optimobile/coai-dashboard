## FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

**[City, State, Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) today announced a strategic partnership with the nation of Madagascar to develop and implement a comprehensive AI Safety Framework. This landmark initiative will position Madagascar as a regional leader in responsible AI adoption and help to ensure that the country is prepared for the opportunities and challenges of the AI revolution.

"We are thrilled to be partnering with Madagascar on this important initiative," said [Name, Title] of CSOAI. "Madagascar has a unique opportunity to build a world-class AI governance structure that will protect its citizens, promote innovation, and drive sustainable and inclusive growth. We are confident that our white-label AI Safety Framework, combined with our professional certification program and proven compliance methodology, will provide Madagascar with the tools it needs to succeed."

The partnership will include the deployment of CSOAI's white-label AI Safety Framework, the training of 1,000 Malagasy AI safety analysts through the Council of European AI Safety Analysts International (CEASAI), and the implementation of CSOAI's 33-Agent Byzantine Council for AI oversight and SOAI-PDCA compliance methodology.

"This partnership is a game-changer for Madagascar," said [Name, Title], a key figure in Madagascar's technology sector. "It will not only help us to bridge the AI governance gap but also to create new job opportunities for our citizens and to position our country as a hub for AI innovation in Africa."

As part of the initiative, CSOAI will also be offering free CEASAI certification to eligible candidates in Madagascar through its £1 Billion Training Giveaway. This global program is designed to train 500,250 AI safety analysts worldwide and to make AI safety education accessible to everyone.
