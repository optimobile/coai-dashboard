**CSOAI Launches Initiative to Bring AI Safety Framework to São Tomé and Príncipe**

**SÃO TOMÉ, SÃO TOMÉ AND PRÍNCIPE – [Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) today announced a major initiative to support São Tomé and Príncipe in developing a national AI safety framework. This move comes as the nation embraces digital transformation and seeks to align with international standards, including the EU's AI Act.

CSOAI will provide its white-label AI Safety Framework, a comprehensive set of policies and standards for responsible AI development and deployment. The initiative also includes free access to the CEASAI certification program for citizens of São Tomé and Príncipe, part of a £1 billion global training giveaway to create a skilled workforce of AI Safety Analysts.

"São Tomé and Príncipe is at a pivotal moment in its technological journey," said [CSOAI Spokesperson Name], [Title] at CSOAI. "By establishing a robust AI safety framework now, the nation can foster innovation, protect its citizens, and build a prosperous digital future. We are committed to providing the tools and training necessary to achieve this."

The implementation of the framework will be guided by a four-phase roadmap, including the establishment of a 33-Agent Byzantine Council for AI oversight and the training of 1,000 local AI Safety Analysts.

## Key Messages

### AI Challenges in São Tomé and Príncipe
- Limited digital infrastructure and a significant skills gap in AI and related technologies.
- Absence of a comprehensive legal and regulatory framework for AI, including specific data protection laws for emerging technologies.
- Nascent private sector adoption of AI, with most current initiatives being government-led.
- Small market size and limited financial resources to invest in large-scale AI development and infrastructure projects.
