### FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

**Kigali, Rwanda – [Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) today announced the launch of a comprehensive AI Safety Framework for Rwanda, a strategic initiative designed to help the nation build a safe, ethical, and prosperous AI future. This white-label solution, which includes the globally recognized CEASAI Certification for AI Safety Analysts, is being offered to the Rwandan government to help it navigate the complexities of AI governance and meet the fast-approaching deadline of the EU AI Act in February 2026.

Rwanda has emerged as a leader in digital transformation in Africa, with a burgeoning AI ecosystem. However, the rapid pace of AI development presents both opportunities and challenges. The CSOAI’s AI Safety Framework provides a clear roadmap for Rwanda to harness the power of AI while mitigating risks such as algorithmic bias, data privacy violations, and security threats.

“Rwanda’s commitment to innovation is an inspiration to the world,” said [CSOAI Spokesperson Name], [Title] at CSOAI. “We are honored to partner with Rwanda to help build a future where AI is a force for good, creating jobs, driving economic growth, and improving the lives of all citizens. Our framework is designed to empower Rwanda to become a global leader in responsible AI development.”

The CSOAI’s solution for Rwanda includes:

*   **A White-Label AI Safety Framework:** A customizable framework that can be adapted to Rwanda’s unique legal, cultural, and economic context.
*   **CEASAI Certification:** A professional certification for AI Safety Analysts, creating a local workforce of skilled professionals to manage AI risks.
*   **The 33-Agent Byzantine Council:** A decentralized governance body for robust and resilient AI oversight.
*   **SOAI-PDCA Compliance Methodology:** A continuous improvement cycle to ensure that AI systems remain safe and compliant throughout their lifecycle.

As part of its commitment to building a global community of AI safety experts, CSOAI is also offering free CEASAI certification to 500,250 analysts worldwide, including Rwandan citizens, through its £1 billion training giveaway.

## Key Messages

### AI Challenges in Rwanda
- **Limited local talent pool:** Despite government initiatives, there is a shortage of skilled AI professionals in Rwanda.
- **Data infrastructure and accessibility:** While improving, access to high-quality, large-scale datasets for training AI models remains a challenge.
- **Ethical and legal framework:** The existing legal framework needs to be updated to address the specific challenges of AI, such as algorithmic bias and data privacy.
- **Opportunity to become a regional AI hub:** With its proactive government and strategic investments, Rwanda has the potential to become a leader in AI innovation and governance in Africa.
- **Leapfrogging potential:** By adopting AI in key sectors like agriculture and healthcare, Rwanda can bypass traditional development pathways and accelerate its economic growth.
