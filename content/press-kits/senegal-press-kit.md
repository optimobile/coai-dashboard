**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**Senegal Partners with CSOAI to Launch National AI Safety Framework**

**DAKAR, Senegal – [Date]** – The Government of Senegal, in a landmark move to secure its digital future, today announced a strategic partnership with the Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) to develop and implement a comprehensive national AI Safety Framework. This initiative will position Senegal as a leader in responsible AI governance in West Africa, ensuring that the development and deployment of artificial intelligence within the country is safe, ethical, and beneficial for all its citizens.

The partnership comes at a critical time, as nations worldwide grapple with the challenges and opportunities presented by the rapid advancement of AI. The AI Safety Framework will provide a clear roadmap for Senegal to navigate the complexities of AI, fostering innovation while mitigating potential risks such as algorithmic bias, data privacy violations, and job displacement.

CSOAI’s white-label AI Safety Framework, which will be customized to Senegal’s unique needs, includes several key components:

*   **CEASAI Certification:** A professional training and certification program to build a local workforce of AI safety experts.
*   **33-Agent Byzantine Council:** An independent oversight body to ensure ethical AI development and deployment.
*   **SOAI-PDCA Compliance Methodology:** A structured approach for continuous improvement of AI safety practices.

“We are honored to partner with the Government of Senegal on this momentous initiative,” said [Name and Title of CSOAI Representative]. “Our mission is to empower nations with the tools and expertise to harness the power of AI responsibly. We are confident that this partnership will not only benefit Senegal but also serve as a model for other countries in the region.”

As part of this collaboration, Senegalese citizens will have access to CSOAI’s £1 billion global training giveaway, which provides free access to the CEASAI certification program. This will create a pipeline of skilled AI safety professionals in Senegal, fostering local innovation and job creation.

## Key Messages

### AI Challenges in Senegal
*   Low R&D investment, hindering innovation and competitiveness.
*   Significant digital divide between urban and rural areas, as well as a notable gender gap in internet usage and STEM fields.
*   The absence of a specific legal and regulatory framework for AI, creating uncertainty and potential risks.
*   Vulnerability to cyber-attacks, necessitating a stronger focus on cybersecurity measures.
