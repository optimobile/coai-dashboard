**Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) Launches Initiative to Establish AI Safety Framework in Algeria**

**[CITY, ALGERIA] – [Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI), a leading global organization in artificial intelligence safety and governance, today announced a major initiative to support Algeria in establishing a national AI safety framework. This move comes as the deadline for compliance with the European Union’s AI Act approaches, highlighting the urgent need for robust AI governance to ensure the safe and ethical development of AI technologies.

Algeria, with its ambitious digital transformation agenda, is at a pivotal moment. The CSOAI’s initiative will provide the Algerian government and private sector with the tools, expertise, and training necessary to build a world-class AI governance framework. This will not only mitigate the risks associated with AI but also unlock its immense potential for economic growth and social development.

“We are thrilled to partner with Algeria to build a future where AI is a force for good,” said [Name, Title] of CSOAI. “Our white-label AI Safety Framework, combined with our comprehensive training and certification programs, will empower Algeria to become a regional leader in responsible AI innovation.”

The CSOAI’s solution for Algeria includes:

*   **A White-Label AI Safety Framework:** A customizable and comprehensive governance structure that aligns with international standards, including the EU AI Act.
*   **CEASAI Certification:** Free access to the Certified Expert in AI Safety and Auditing (CEASAI) program, with the goal of training 1,000 AI Safety Analysts in Algeria.
*   **The 33-Agent Byzantine Council:** A state-of-the-art AI-powered oversight body to ensure the integrity and resilience of the AI governance framework.
*   **SOAI-PDCA Compliance Methodology:** A continuous improvement cycle to keep the framework effective and up-to-date.

This initiative is part of CSOAI’s £1 billion global training giveaway, which aims to democratize access to AI safety education and create a global network of AI safety experts.
