**[City, DRC] – [Date]** – The Council of Safe AI (CSOAI) today announced a major initiative to support the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) in building a safe and ethical AI ecosystem. The initiative includes a comprehensive AI Safety Framework, a £1 billion training giveaway, and the establishment of a national AI oversight council.

This announcement comes as the DRC is embarking on an ambitious digital transformation journey, with the government recently launching a $1.5 billion digital plan to become a regional tech hub by 2030. The plan includes the country's first National Artificial Intelligence Strategy, signaling a strong commitment to harnessing the power of AI for national development.

"The Democratic Republic of Congo has a unique opportunity to become a leader in responsible AI adoption in Africa," said [Name, Title] of CSOAI. "Our comprehensive solution is designed to empower the DRC to build a future where AI drives economic growth, improves public services, and creates a prosperous future for all its citizens, while mitigating the risks associated with this transformative technology."

CSOAI's solution for the DRC includes:

*   **A White-label AI Safety Framework:** A customizable, ready-to-deploy framework that can be adapted to the DRC's unique legal, cultural, and economic landscape.
*   **The CEASAI Certification:** A world-class training and certification program that will equip Congolese professionals with the skills and knowledge to become certified AI Safety Analysts.
*   **The 33-Agent Byzantine Council for AI:** An innovative governance model for robust and resilient AI oversight.
*   **The SOAI-PDCA Compliance Methodology:** A continuous improvement cycle for organizations to assess their AI systems, identify and mitigate risks, and continuously improve their AI safety and governance practices.

To jumpstart the development of a skilled AI safety workforce in the DRC, CSOAI is also launching a £1 billion training giveaway, offering free access to its CEASAI certification program. This initiative is part of a global effort to train 500,250 AI Safety Analysts worldwide.

## Key Messages

### AI Challenges in Congo (DRC)
- Limited digital infrastructure and internet connectivity, hindering widespread AI adoption.
- A shortage of skilled AI professionals and data scientists to develop and manage AI systems.
- The absence of a comprehensive legal and regulatory framework to govern AI development and deployment, creating uncertainty and risk.
- Opportunities to leverage AI in key sectors like mining for exploration and operational efficiency, in agriculture to improve crop yields and food security, and in healthcare to enhance diagnostics and treatment.
