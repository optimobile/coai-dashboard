# EU AI Act Fundamentals Course - Complete Content Summary

## Course Overview

**Course Title**: EU AI Act Fundamentals  
**Level**: Fundamentals  
**Duration**: 8-10 hours  
**Target Audience**: AI professionals, compliance officers, product managers, legal teams, business leaders  
**Prerequisites**: None  
**Certification**: Upon completion and passing the final exam

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, learners will be able to:

1. Explain the purpose, scope, and regulatory framework of the EU AI Act
2. Classify AI systems by risk level (prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, minimal-risk)
3. Identify prohibited AI practices and understand enforcement timeline
4. Recognize all eight categories of high-risk AI systems (Annex III)
5. Distinguish between provider and deployer roles and obligations
6. Apply transparency requirements to all AI systems
7. Understand General-Purpose AI (GPAI) regulation
8. Navigate the compliance timeline and penalty structure
9. Develop an organizational compliance roadmap

## Module Structure

### Module 1: Introduction to the EU AI Act
**Duration**: 45 minutes  
**Content**: 3,000 words

**Topics**:
- Why the EU AI Act exists
- Scope and applicability
- Risk-based regulatory framework
- Key definitions and concepts
- Relationship to other EU laws (GDPR, Product Safety, etc.)

**Key Takeaways**:
- The Act applies to providers and deployers of AI systems in the EU
- Risk-based approach: higher risk = stricter requirements
- Extraterritorial reach (like GDPR)
- Entered into force August 1, 2024

### Module 2: Risk Classification System
**Duration**: 45 minutes  
**Content**: 3,500 words

**Topics**:
- Four risk categories (prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, minimal-risk)
- How to classify your AI systems
- Annex I (safety components) and Annex III (use cases)
- Exceptions and edge cases

**Key Takeaways**:
- Prohibited AI is banned outright
- High-risk AI faces extensive compliance obligations
- Limited-risk AI requires transparency
- Minimal-risk AI has no specific obligations
- Classification determines compliance requirements

### Module 3: Prohibited AI Practices
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Content**: 5,000 words

**Topics**:
- All eight prohibited practices (Article 5)
- Subliminal manipulation and exploitation of vulnerabilities
- Social scoring by public authorities
- Biometric categorization of sensitive attributes
- Predictive policing based solely on profiling
- Untargeted scraping of facial images
- Emotion recognition in workplaces and schools
- Real-time biometric identification in public spaces
- Exceptions and enforcement timeline (February 2, 2025)

**Key Takeaways**:
- Prohibited practices must be eliminated by February 2, 2025
- No compliance pathway—just cessation
- Highest penalties (€35M or 7% turnover)
- Narrow exceptions for law enforcement biometric identification

### Module 4: High-Risk AI Systems - Part 1
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Content**: 5,500 words

**Topics**:
- Annex III Categories 1-4:
  1. Biometric identification and categorization
  2. Critical infrastructure (transport, water, gas, electricity, etc.)
  3. Education and vocational training
  4. Employment and worker management
- Real-world examples and case studies
- Exceptions (narrow procedural tasks)

**Key Takeaways**:
- Biometric identification (not verification) is high-risk
- Critical infrastructure AI affects safety
- Educational AI affects life opportunities
- Employment AI affects livelihoods and rights
- Narrow procedural tasks are exempt

### Module 5: High-Risk AI Systems - Part 2
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Content**: 5,500 words

**Topics**:
- Annex III Categories 5-8:
  5. Essential private and public services (credit scoring, emergency dispatching)
  6. Law enforcement (seven specific use cases)
  7. Migration, asylum, and border control
  8. Administration of justice and democratic processes
- Heightened sensitivity of these categories
- Practical application and case studies

**Key Takeaways**:
- Credit scoring is high-risk (fraud detection exempt)
- Emergency dispatching is high-risk (life-and-death decisions)
- Law enforcement AI has seven specific high-risk categories
- Migration AI affects safety and family unity
- Judicial AI requires human decision-making

### Module 6: Provider and Deployer Obligations
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Content**: 5,500 words

**Topics**:
- Defining providers and deployers
- Provider obligations (12 requirements):
  1. Risk management system
  2. Data and data governance
  3. Technical documentation
  4. Record-keeping and logging
  5. Transparency to deployers
  6. Human oversight
  7. Accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity
  8. Quality management system
  9. Conformity assessment
  10. Registration
  11. Post-market monitoring
  12. Incident reporting
- Deployer obligations (7 requirements)
- Practical compliance roadmaps

**Key Takeaways**:
- Providers bear primary compliance responsibility
- Deployers must use systems properly and implement oversight
- Substantial modification makes deployers providers
- Compliance requires systematic, organization-wide processes
- Conformity assessment required before market entry

### Module 7: Transparency, GPAI, and Governance
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Content**: 5,000 words

**Topics**:
- Transparency obligations for all AI systems:
  * AI interaction disclosure (chatbots)
  * Emotion recognition/biometric categorization disclosure
  * Deep fake disclosure
- General-Purpose AI (GPAI) regulation:
  * Baseline obligations (all GPAI)
  * Additional obligations (systemic risk GPAI)
  * Compute threshold (10^25 FLOPs)
- Governance structure:
  * AI Office
  * European AI Board
  * National competent authorities
  * Notified bodies
  * Advisory Forum
- Codes of practice

**Key Takeaways**:
- All AI systems must disclose their nature
- GPAI providers must document and share information
- Systemic risk GPAI faces additional obligations
- Multi-layered governance structure
- Codes of practice provide compliance guidance

### Module 8: Compliance Timeline, Penalties, and Next Steps
**Duration**: 60 minutes  
**Content**: 5,000 words

**Topics**:
- Phased implementation timeline:
  * February 2, 2025: Prohibited practices
  * August 2, 2025: GPAI codes + transparency
  * August 2, 2026: High-risk + GPAI
  * August 2, 2027: Legacy high-risk systems
- Penalty structure (three tiers)
- Enforcement mechanisms
- Immediate compliance actions (6 steps)
- Long-term compliance roadmaps
- Resources and next steps

**Key Takeaways**:
- Staggered deadlines over 36 months
- Severe penalties (up to €35M or 7% turnover)
- Start compliance efforts now
- Prioritize prohibited practices, then high-risk systems
- Compliance is ongoing, not one-time
