## Executive Summary

The CEASA Public Authority Dashboard provides comprehensive transparency into CEASA operations, analyst certification, job market data, and AI safety incident analysis. The dashboard serves multiple stakeholders: regulators monitoring CEASA compliance, employers seeking certified analysts, analysts tracking career progress, and the public understanding AI safety governance.

**Dashboard Audiences:** Regulators, Employers, Analysts, Researchers, Civil Society, General Public  
**Data Categories:** Certification statistics, job market data, incident analysis, financial performance, governance transparency  
**Update Frequency:** Real-time for most metrics, daily for aggregated data, monthly for detailed reports  
**Public Access:** 100% of dashboard publicly accessible (no login required)

## Part 1: Public Transparency Framework

### 1.1 Transparency Principles

**CEASA Transparency Commitments:**

**Openness:** All CEASA data, decisions, and procedures are publicly accessible except where confidentiality is required (personal data, proprietary information).

**Accountability:** CEASA is accountable to regulators, employers, analysts, and the public for maintaining high standards of competence, impartiality, and quality.

**Accessibility:** All public data is presented in accessible formats (web, PDF, CSV, API) with clear explanations and visualizations.

**Timeliness:** Data is updated regularly (real-time for some metrics, daily for others) to provide current information.

**Completeness:** All material information about CEASA operations, certifications, and impact is publicly disclosed.

**Accuracy:** All published data is verified, audited, and corrected if errors are discovered.

### 1.2 Data Categories & Disclosure

**Certification Data (Public):**
- Total number of certified analysts by tier
- Certification statistics by specialization
- Certification statistics by region/country
- Pass rates and performance trends
- Demographic diversity of certified analysts
- Certification renewal rates

**Job Market Data (Public):**
- Total job opportunities posted
- Job market trends and growth
- Salary ranges by tier and specialization
- Job placement rates
- Employer satisfaction metrics
- Industry distribution of jobs

**Incident Analysis Data (Public):**
- Total AI incidents analyzed by CEASA analysts
- Incident categories and trends
- Incident severity distribution
- Incident resolution outcomes
- Lessons learned and recommendations
- Systemic risk assessment

**Financial Data (Public):**
- Annual revenue and expenses
- Certification fee structure
- Accreditation costs
- Administrative costs
- Investment in research and development
- Financial sustainability metrics

**Governance Data (Public):**
- Board member names and affiliations
- Board meeting schedule and minutes
- Curriculum changes and rationale
- Advisory board decisions
- Accreditation body assessments
- Regulatory compliance status

**Analyst Data (Opt-In Public):**
- Public directory of certified analysts (opt-in)
- Analyst name, tier, specializations
- Analyst location and contact information
- Analyst professional background
- Analyst areas of expertise
- Analyst availability for consulting

**Confidential Data (Not Public):**
- Individual exam scores and responses
- Candidate identity (except public directory opt-ins)
- Employer proprietary information
- Personal financial information
- Medical or disability information
- Internal personnel matters

## Part 3: Analyst Public Directory

### 3.1 Public Directory (Opt-In)

**Directory Purpose:**
- Enable employers to find certified analysts
- Enable analysts to build professional reputation
- Enable public to verify analyst credentials
- Support analyst career advancement

**Directory Access:**
- Public web directory at https://directory.ceasa.eu
- Search by name, location, specialization, tier
- Filter by availability, experience, industry
- Analyst profiles with credentials and experience

**Analyst Profile Information (Opt-In):**
- Name and professional title
- Certification tier and specializations
- Location and work preferences
- Professional background and experience
- Areas of expertise
- Contact information (email, phone, website)
- Professional photo
- LinkedIn profile link
- Availability for consulting/employment
- Languages spoken
- Industries of expertise
- Publications and thought leadership
- Speaking engagements and presentations

**Privacy & Control:**
- Analysts choose what information to display
- Opt-in to public directory
- Update profile information anytime
- Remove from directory anytime
- Control which information is searchable
- Block specific employers or contacts

**Verification:**
- CEASA verifies all listed credentials
- Certification status displayed
- Renewal status displayed
- Specialization status displayed
- Verification badge on profile
