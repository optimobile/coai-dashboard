# NIST AI Risk Management Framework - Course Structure

## Course Progression Overview

The NIST AI RMF training program consists of three progressive levels designed to build comprehensive expertise in AI risk management:

1. **NIST AI RMF Fundamentals** (8 modules, ~20 hours) - Foundation-level understanding
2. **NIST AI RMF Advanced** (10 modules, ~30 hours) - Implementation and organizational integration
3. **NIST AI RMF Specialist** (10 modules, ~40 hours) - Expert-level risk management and governance

---

## Course 1: NIST AI RMF Fundamentals

**Target Audience**: AI practitioners, compliance officers, risk managers, product managers, anyone new to AI risk management

**Prerequisites**: Basic understanding of AI/ML concepts (recommended but not required)

**Duration**: 20 hours

**Learning Objectives**:
By the end of this course, learners will be able to:
- Explain the purpose and structure of the NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- Identify and describe the seven characteristics of trustworthy AI systems
- Understand the four core functions: Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage
- Recognize AI risks and their potential impacts on individuals, organizations, and society
- Apply basic risk management concepts to AI systems
- Describe the roles and responsibilities of AI actors across the lifecycle
- Implement foundational risk management practices for AI systems

### Module Structure

#### Module 1: Introduction to NIST AI RMF (2.5 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand the history and development of the NIST AI RMF
- Explain why AI risk management is critical for trustworthy AI
- Identify the voluntary nature and benefits of the framework
- Describe how AI RMF aligns with other frameworks (EU AI Act, ISO 42001)
- Recognize the stakeholders and their roles in AI risk management

**Topics**:
- Background and context of AI RMF development
- The need for AI risk management
- Framework structure and components
- Relationship to other AI governance frameworks
- Key terminology and definitions
- Real-world examples of AI risks

#### Module 2: Trustworthy AI Characteristics (3 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Define and explain all seven trustworthy AI characteristics
- Understand the interdependencies between characteristics
- Recognize tradeoffs between different trustworthy characteristics
- Apply trustworthiness principles to real-world AI systems
- Evaluate AI systems based on trustworthiness criteria

**Topics**:
- **Valid and Reliable**: Accuracy, robustness, generalizability
- **Safe**: Safety considerations across AI lifecycle
- **Secure and Resilient**: Cybersecurity and adversarial robustness
- **Accountable and Transparent**: Provenance, documentation, disclosure
- **Explainable and Interpretable**: Mechanisms and meanings of AI outputs
- **Privacy-Enhanced**: Privacy-enhancing technologies and data minimization
- **Fair with Harmful Bias Managed**: Three categories of AI bias
- Balancing tradeoffs between characteristics
- Case studies: Trustworthy vs. untrustworthy AI systems

#### Module 3: GOVERN Function (2.5 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Establish organizational structures for AI risk management
- Define roles and responsibilities for AI governance
- Create policies and procedures for AI risk management
- Build a culture of responsible AI development
- Implement accountability mechanisms

**Topics**:
- Organizational governance structures
- AI risk management policies and procedures
- Roles and responsibilities (AI designers, developers, deployers, users)
- Stakeholder engagement and communication
- Accountability and oversight mechanisms
- Governance case studies from leading organizations

#### Module 4: MAP Function (2.5 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify and document AI system context and intended use
- Categorize AI systems based on risk levels
- Map potential risks, impacts, and harms
- Document stakeholders and their concerns
- Create comprehensive AI system profiles

**Topics**:
- AI system context documentation
- Risk categorization methodologies
- Identifying potential harms and impacts
- Stakeholder analysis and engagement
- Creating AI system profiles
- Mapping exercises with real-world examples

#### Module 5: MEASURE Function (2.5 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Select appropriate metrics for AI risk assessment
- Employ quantitative and qualitative measurement methods
- Evaluate trustworthiness characteristics using metrics
- Track AI system performance over time
- Use measurement tools and methodologies

**Topics**:
- Quantitative vs. qualitative risk assessment methods
- Metrics for trustworthiness characteristics
- Testing and validation methodologies
- Continuous monitoring approaches
- Benchmarking and baseline establishment
- Measurement tools and platforms

#### Module 6: MANAGE Function (2.5 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Prioritize AI risks based on impact and likelihood
- Allocate resources to risk treatment
- Implement risk mitigation strategies
- Document risk management decisions
- Respond to and recover from AI incidents

**Topics**:
- Risk prioritization frameworks
- Risk treatment options (avoid, mitigate, transfer, accept)
- Resource allocation for risk management
- Risk mitigation strategies and controls
- Incident response and recovery
- Continuous improvement cycles

#### Module 7: AI Lifecycle and Actors (2 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand the complete AI lifecycle from design to retirement
- Identify roles and responsibilities at each lifecycle stage
- Recognize the importance of cross-functional collaboration
- Apply risk management practices throughout the lifecycle
- Engage stakeholders effectively at each stage

**Topics**:
- AI lifecycle stages: planning, data collection, model building, validation, deployment, operation, retirement
- AI actors: designers, developers, deployers, end users, subject matter experts
- Cross-functional collaboration and communication
- Lifecycle-specific risk management practices
- Stakeholder engagement strategies
- Documentation and handoffs between lifecycle stages

#### Module 8: Implementation Roadmap and Best Practices (2.5 hours)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Create an AI risk management implementation plan
- Apply NIST AI RMF to real-world scenarios
- Recognize common challenges and solutions
- Leverage best practices from industry leaders
- Prepare for certification exam

**Topics**:
- Step-by-step implementation roadmap
- Quick wins and early actions
- Common implementation challenges and solutions
- Industry best practices and case studies
- Integration with existing risk management processes
- Resources and tools for ongoing learning
- Course review and exam preparation
