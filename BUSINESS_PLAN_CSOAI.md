## Problem Statement

### The Regulatory Crisis

On February 2, 2026, the EU AI Act becomes enforceable. Every enterprise deploying AI systems must comply with strict requirements:

- Risk assessment and documentation
- Human oversight mechanisms
- Transparency and explainability
- Regular audits and compliance reporting
- Enforcement by EU member states

**Impact**: 50,000 EU enterprises need immediate compliance solutions.

### The Analyst Shortage

Each enterprise needs 2-5 AI Safety Analysts to:

- Conduct risk assessments
- Design compliance procedures
- Oversee AI system deployments
- Conduct audits
- Report to regulators

**Gap**: 100,000-250,000 analysts needed. Zero currently exist.

### The Employment Crisis

Millions of people want meaningful remote work. AI Safety Analyst positions offer:

- $45-150/hour compensation
- 100% remote work
- Meaningful impact on AI safety
- Career growth and advancement
- Global opportunities

**Opportunity**: Create 250,000 new jobs while solving regulatory crisis.
