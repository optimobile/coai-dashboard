## Framework 1: EU AI Act Compliance

### Regulatory Source

The **EU AI Act** (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) is the primary regulatory framework for artificial intelligence in the European Union. It became enforceable on February 2, 2026.

### Exam Coverage

**Fundamentals Level** (50 questions, 4 hours)

The Fundamentals exam covers core EU AI Act concepts including risk classification, prohibited practices, high-risk system requirements, and enforcement mechanisms.

**Key Topics Verified**:
- Risk classification system (prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, minimal-risk)
- Prohibited AI practices (social scoring, real-time biometric identification in public spaces)
- High-risk AI systems (Annex III: employment, education, law enforcement, critical infrastructure)
- Provider obligations (documentation, testing, monitoring, human oversight)
- Deployer obligations (compliance assessment, documentation, human oversight)
- Transparency requirements (disclosure of AI use, content generation disclosure)
- Penalties and enforcement (up to €30M or 6% of global revenue)

**Question Verification**: All 50 questions verified against EU AI Act text and official guidance documents.

- Supply chain compliance
