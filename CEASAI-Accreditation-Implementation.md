## Part 1: ISO 17024:2012 Requirements & Implementation

### 1.1 ISO 17024 Overview

ISO/IEC 17024:2012 is the international standard for **personnel certification bodies**. It establishes requirements for organizations that certify individuals based on competence assessments. The standard ensures:

- **Competence** - Certification bodies have technical expertise and knowledge
- **Consistency** - Standardized procedures and fair assessment
- **Impartiality** - Independence from conflicts of interest
- **Credibility** - Recognized and trusted certifications globally

### 1.2 ISO 17024 Core Requirements

#### **A. Organizational Requirements**

**Legal Status & Independence:**
- [ ] CEASA established as independent legal entity or organizational unit
- [ ] Governance structure with clear decision-making authority
- [ ] Separation from training providers (to ensure impartiality)
- [ ] Conflict-of-interest management procedures
- [ ] Documented organizational chart and responsibilities

**Impartiality & Conflicts of Interest:**
- [ ] Impartiality statement and policy
- [ ] Conflict-of-interest declaration procedures
- [ ] Procedures to prevent conflicts affecting certification decisions
- [ ] Restrictions on CEASA staff certifying themselves
- [ ] Restrictions on staff with financial interests in candidates

**Complaints & Appeals:**
- [ ] Documented complaints procedure
- [ ] Appeals process for exam results and certification decisions
- [ ] Independent appeals committee
- [ ] Timeline for complaint resolution (30 days)
- [ ] Record-keeping of all complaints and appeals

**Financial & Insurance:**
- [ ] Adequate financial resources for operations
- [ ] Professional liability insurance
- [ ] Contingency planning for business continuity
- [ ] Financial reserves for certificate replacement/reissuance

#### **B. Competence Requirements**

**Technical Committee:**
- [ ] Establish Technical Committee with subject matter experts
- [ ] Committee members have minimum 5 years AI safety experience
- [ ] Committee includes practitioners, academics, and regulators
- [ ] Committee meets quarterly to review curriculum and standards
- [ ] Committee approves all exam questions and assessment procedures

**Staff Competence:**
- [ ] Exam development specialists with assessment expertise
- [ ] Grading and assessment specialists
- [ ] IT and security specialists for exam administration
- [ ] Continuing education in AI safety and EU AI Act
- [ ] Annual competence assessment for all staff

**Subject Matter Experts:**
- [ ] Minimum 5 years professional experience in AI safety
- [ ] Demonstrated expertise in relevant domain
- [ ] Active participation in industry and research
- [ ] Continuing education and professional development
- [ ] Annual competence verification

#### **C. Certification Scheme Requirements**

- [ ] Defined competence requirements and performance standards
- [ ] Prerequisite and progression requirements
- [ ] Specialization track definitions

- [ ] Renewal and recertification procedures

**Competence Standards:**

**Specialization Tracks:**

## Part 3: Accreditation Body Selection & Strategy

### 3.1 Primary Accreditation Bodies

**For ISO 17024 (Personnel Certification):**

| Accreditation Body | Coverage | Timeline | Notes |
|-------------------|----------|----------|-------|
| **PJLA** (Professional Testing Laboratory Association) | US-based, international recognition | 6-9 months | Specializes in personnel certification |
| **A2LA** (American Association for Laboratory Accreditation) | US-based, international recognition | 6-9 months | Broad accreditation experience |
| **ANAB** (ANSI National Accreditation Board) | US-based, international recognition | 6-9 months | Largest US accreditation body |
| **ILAC** (International Laboratory Accreditation Cooperation) | International mutual recognition | 12+ months | Highest international credibility |

**For ISO 17065 (Product Certification):**

| Accreditation Body | Coverage | Timeline | Notes |
|-------------------|----------|----------|-------|
| **A2LA** | US-based, international recognition | 9-12 months | Product certification experience |
| **IAS** (International Accreditation Service) | US-based, international recognition | 9-12 months | Specializes in product certification |
| **NANDO** (EU Notified Bodies) | EU regulatory recognition | 12-18 months | Required for EU regulatory authority |

### 3.2 Recommended Accreditation Pathway

**Phase 1: ISO 17024 with PJLA (Months 6-12)**
- PJLA has strong experience with personnel certification
- Faster accreditation timeline (6-9 months)
- Recognized internationally
- Allows CEASA to begin certifying analysts while pursuing ISO 17065

**Phase 2: ISO 17065 with A2LA (Months 18-24)**
- A2LA has dual accreditation experience
- Can leverage existing ISO 17024 accreditation
- Recognized for product certification
- Prepares for EU regulatory recognition

**Phase 3: NANDO Notified Body Status (Months 24-36)**
- Apply for EU regulatory recognition
- Requires both ISO 17024 and ISO 17065
- Positions CEASA as EU authority
- Enables certification of AI systems under EU AI Act
